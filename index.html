The privacy policy for Trezor, accessible via trezor.io, outlines how they handle personal data. Key points include:

  1. Data Collection:

    • Website and Services: Trezor collects and processes personal data to enable the use of their website and services, including customer support. This data collection is justified under Article 6 (1) (b) of the GDPR, necessary for contract performance, and Article 6 (1) (f), legitimate interests.
    • Newsletter: When you subscribe to the Trezor newsletter, your email is collected to send you updates. You can unsubscribe anytime.
    • Trezor Shop: When ordering from the Trezor Shop, data like delivery address, email, and order status are collected to ensure proper delivery and legal compliance.

  2. Payment Gateways: Payment data is processed by third-party gateways, which have their privacy policies. Trezor does not have access to this data.

  3. Data Retention: Personal data is stored only as long as necessary for its processing purpose, to comply with legal obligations, or until consent is withdrawn. Data from shop transactions are anonymized three months after the sale.

  4. Data Security: Trezor employs standard security practices to protect personal data against unauthorized access, loss, or misuse. They collaborate with partners who must also ensure data security.

  5. Data Sharing: Data may be shared with technology service providers, security and integrity testers, accounting and legal service providers, and Trezor staff. In certain circumstances, data may be shared with public authorities or transmitted to third countries, compliant with the GDPR and the Privacy Shield program.

For more detailed information, you can visit the Trezor privacy policy page (Trezor Wallet) (Trezor).
